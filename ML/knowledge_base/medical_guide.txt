MEDI-SCAN MEDICAL GUIDELINES V1

1. DIABETES MANAGEMENT
- Definition: A chronic condition that affects how the body turns food into energy.
- High Risk Indicators: Fasting Glucose > 126 mg/dL or HbA1c > 6.5%.
- Lifestyle Advice: Reduce refined sugars (soda, candy). Increase fiber intake (vegetables, whole grains). Exercise at least 30 minutes a day.
- Warning Signs: Frequent urination, excessive thirst, blurred vision.

2. KIDNEY HEALTH (CKD)
- Definition: Chronic Kidney Disease means kidneys are damaged and can't filter blood properly.
- High Risk Indicators: Creatinine > 1.2 mg/dL (Women) or > 1.4 mg/dL (Men). eGFR < 60.
- Lifestyle Advice: Hydration is key, but don't overhydrate if you have late-stage CKD. Limit sodium (salt) to control blood pressure. Avoid NSAIDs (painkillers like Ibuprofen).

3. LIVER FUNCTION
- Definition: The liver processes blood and breaks down, balances, and creates the nutrients.
- High Risk Indicators: Elevated ALT/AST enzymes, high Bilirubin (Jaundice).
- Lifestyle Advice: Strictly avoid alcohol. Lose weight if overweight to prevent Fatty Liver. Avoid processed foods high in trans fats.

4. THYROID DISORDERS
- Hypothyroidism (Underactive): High TSH, Low T4. Symptoms: Fatigue, weight gain, cold sensitivity.
- Hyperthyroidism (Overactive): Low TSH, High T4. Symptoms: Anxiety, weight loss, rapid heartbeat.
- Advice: Thyroid medication must be taken on an empty stomach. Iodine intake should be regulated.

5. ANEMIA
- Definition: Lack of healthy red blood cells to carry oxygen.
- Indicators: Hemoglobin < 13.0 (Men) or < 12.0 (Women).
- Advice: Eat iron-rich foods (spinach, red meat, lentils). Vitamin C helps iron absorption.
